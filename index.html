About

SDG-9

Sustainable Development Goal 9 emphasizes the importance of building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation. It aims to bridge disparities in development, boost economic growth, and drive global progress while safeguarding environmental sustainability.

App

The app serves as a platform to raise awareness, inspire action, and foster collaboration towards achieving Sustainable Development Goal 9. It educates users about resilient infrastructure, sustainable industrialization, and innovation through resources, data, and visualizations. The app showcases success stories, highlights global challenges, and connects individuals, organizations, and policymakers. By providing tools for idea sharing and actionable steps, the app empowers users to contribute to sustainable development and promote economic growth while preserving the environment.

Key statistics

-Over 1 billion people lack reliable infrastructure, impeding their development opportunities.

-60% of CO2 emissions come from industrial operations, urging a shift to green practices.

-Nearly 50% of small-scale industries lack access to financial services, slowing their growth.

-Global R&D spending accounts for only 2.3% of GDP, highlighting a gap in innovation.

Renewable resources are resources that can replenish themselves or continue to exist even when being consumed. They are considered to have a lesser negative impact on the environment than nonrenewable resources.

Solar energy: The most abundant energy resource, which can be used even when it's cloudy. However, it's not always available, such as at night or when it's overcast.

Wind energy: Wind turbines generate electricity by converting the kinetic energy of the spinning blades into electric energy. Wind energy has fewer environmental effects than many other energy sources.

Hydroelectric power, or hydropower, is a renewable energy source that uses the kinetic energy of flowing water to generate electricity. Dams and diversion structures are used to alter the natural flow of water, which creates an elevation difference that powers the process: Water flow: Water flows through a pipe, or penstock, inside the dam. Turbine: The moving water turns a turbine propeller at the end of the penstock. Generator: The shaft from the turbine turns a metal shaft in an electric generator, which produces electricity. Electrical grid: The electricity is fed into the electrical grid to power homes, businesses, and industries.

This is my model of an Autonomous Rail Rapid Transit (ART) system. It demonstrates how ART operates using sensors to detect nearby objects, ensuring safety and efficiency in transit. The system uses advanced technology, including virtual tracks guided by GPS and sensors, eliminating the need for physical rails. When the sensor detects an object nearby, it sends a signal to stop the vechicle. This model highlights the innovative features of ARTs, such as eco-friendliness, flexibility, and advanced technology for modern transportation.

Kite Wind-Based Energy Project harnesses the power of high-altitude winds using a specially designed kite. As the kite moves in the sky, it captures wind energy and converts it into mechanical energy through its tethered motion. This energy is then transmitted to a ground station, where it is converted into electricity. The system is inspired by Benjamin Franklinâ€™s historic kite experiment and modern advancements in airborne wind energy. It demonstrates an innovative, sustainable approach to generating electricity, showcasing how high-altitude winds can complement traditional wind turbines to achieve renewable energy goals.

This model demonstrates the process of producing and purifying biogas from organic waste. It begins in the biodigester, where waste decomposes anaerobically to produce raw biogas. The gas is then passed through a water scrubber to remove impurities like carbon dioxide and hydrogen sulfide. An intermediate chamber regulates pressure, and the purified biogas is stored in the final chamber, ready for use. This project highlights how biogas can serve as a clean, renewable energy source while reducing waste and environmental impact. It's a practical solution for sustainable energy production.
